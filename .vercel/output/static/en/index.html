Frequently asked questions

Find clear answers about how we work. We are available for any other questions you may have.

We work with children and adolescents from 0 to 18 years old. Depending on developmental stage, individual characteristics, and specific needs, we provide personalized care adapted to each stage.

The duration of the assessment process depends on both family availability and the child/adolescent psychologist conducting the evaluation. It usually involves five to six visits over about one month.

The frequency of intervention sessions is adjusted to family availability and the child's specific needs, and may be weekly, biweekly, or monthly.

Session cost varies depending on duration, service type, and goals for each visit. During the first contact call, we provide a clear and detailed explanation of applicable fees.

Family involvement is highly valued throughout assessment and intervention. In early intervention, parents actively participate in sessions. For adolescents, we adapt to the needs of both the young person and the family context.

At the moment, some sessions in natural settings such as home or school are offered occasionally. As the team grows, we will expand services and include more frequent interventions in natural environments.
